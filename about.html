About


My name is Keith O'Hara. I am a Ph.D. Candidate at NYU's Department of Economics. My primary research topics are Econometrics and Computational Methods.

In the computational world, I am the author of several C++ libraries and R packages in the areas of:

  • Macroeconometrics
  • Optimal Transport and models of matching
  • Statistics
  • Nonlinear optimization
  • Bayesian simulation methods
  • Computation of special functions

Other Open Source projects that I like and, on occasion, contribute to:

  • The R Project
    • macOS developers: consider joining the dedicated development list.
    • A new group, started by John C. Nash, and supported by the R Consortium, is revisiting many of R's core algorithms. Checkout our progress here and consider joining!
  • Armadillo C++ linear algebra library
    • An expression template library for high performance, matrix-based computation.
    • R wrapper.
